## 📖 Overview
**TEG Simulator Pro** is a professional-grade engineering web application that simulates natural gas dehydration processes using **Triethylene Glycol (TEG)**. This simulator is designed to be a fast, independent, and open-source alternative to complex commercial software like Aspen HYSYS, while strictly maintaining the accuracy of thermodynamic calculations, mass conservation laws, and energy balances.

The application runs entirely in the browser (Client-side) without the need for backend servers, ensuring absolute responsiveness, zero latency, and complete data privacy.

---

## ✨ Key Features (Engineering & Software)

### 🔬 Thermodynamic Core
* **Strict Mass Conservation:** Precise algorithms ensure absolutely no mass is lost or artificially created (Mass IN = Mass OUT) down to the fourth decimal place across all unit operations.
* **Targeted VLE Flash:** Accurate dynamic simulation of the Free Water Knockout (FWKO) separator. It prevents the false condensation of light gases (C1-C4) while properly condensing free water and heavy hydrocarbons based on dynamic $K$-Values.
* **Absorption Simulation (Modified Kremser):** Custom algorithms to simulate the physical absorption of heavy hydrocarbons and aromatic compounds (BTEX) into the glycol within the contactor tower.
* **Accurate Energy Balance:** Precise enthalpy calculations based on the polynomial integration of ideal gas heat capacities ($C_p$), correctly incorporating the latent heat of vaporization/condensation for water streams.

### 💻 UI & Tools
* **SCADA Theme:** A dark, industrial user interface inspired by real-world DCS (Distributed Control Systems) and SCADA monitoring screens.
* **Interactive PFD:** A dynamic Process Flow Diagram displaying gas and glycol routes with real-time SVG animations and instant updates for flow rates and temperatures.
* **Excel Reporting:** Generate comprehensive, production-ready engineering reports detailing material and energy balances for every equipment piece in `.xlsx` format with a single click.
* **OPEX & CO2 Calculator:** Instant calculation of daily operational expenditures (fuel gas consumption and glycol makeup costs) alongside estimated carbon footprint emissions.
* **Smart Assistant:** A built-in bilingual chatbot (English/Arabic) designed to act as a virtual senior engineer, helping users troubleshoot operational issues (e.g., foaming, thermal degradation) and suggesting optimizations based on live simulation data.

## 🏗️ Project Structure

The application is built using **Modular Vanilla JavaScript** to ensure maximum performance, ease of deployment, and straightforward maintenance:

* `index.html`: The main entry point and structure of the Graphical User Interface (UI).
* `style.css`: The SCADA theme stylesheets, layout grids, and SVG pipeline flow animations.
* `engine.js`: **The beating heart of the simulator.** Contains the core OOP thermodynamic classes (`ThermoEngine`, `Stream`, `UnitOperation`, `PlantSimulator`).
* `admin.js`: The dynamic database managing the thermodynamic properties and heat capacity constants ($C_p$).
* `lang.js`: The Bilingual Engine handling UI translations dynamically.
* `chatbot.js`: The NLP logic and engineering knowledge base for the Smart Assistant.

## ⚙️ Supported Unit Operations
1. **Saturator Mixer:** Saturates the feed gas with water to simulate worst-case scenario water loading.
2. **FWKO Separator:** Free Water Knock Out vessel to mechanically separate free liquid water and heavy hydrocarbon condensates.
3. **Absorber T-100:** The contactor tower simulating counter-current mass transfer between wet gas and lean TEG.
4. **L/R Heat Exchanger:** Lean/Rich heat exchanger for maximum energy recovery and thermal shock prevention.
5. **TEG Regenerator:** The distillation column and reboiler simulating the thermal stripping of water to produce 99%+ Lean TEG.
6. **Makeup Mixer:** The unit compensating for minor mechanical and vaporization TEG losses.

## 🚀 Installation & Usage
This application requires zero installation of complex runtime environments (No Node.js, Python, or Docker required). 

1. **Clone or Download** the repository as a ZIP file.
2. Extract the files into a single local directory.
3. Double-click the `index.html` file to open it in any modern web browser (Google Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Mozilla Firefox).
4. Input your plant's operating parameters (Gas Flow, Feed Temp, Target Dew Point, etc.).
5. Click **▶ Run HYSYS Simulation** to instantly generate the results.
